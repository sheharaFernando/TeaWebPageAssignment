HISTORY OF CEYLON TEA

Foundation for Tea Plantation

In order to other countries "Ceylon Tea" is the best rated TEA in the world. In ancient times we haven't had an Tea plants planted in Sri Lanka. With the arrival of Portugese,British and Dutch people our country had the opportunity to plant "Tea" because of the wide areas wich was suits to plant Tea. Nowadays most of the people used to plant Tea in Sri lanka because of the cooling climate in Nuwara Eliya District and it has become the Sri Lanka's largest export industry for over centuries. In 1852 James Tailor arrived to Sri Lanka and he settled down at the Loolecondera Estate which situated in Galaha.He started to plant Tea in the Loolecondera Estate using 19acres from the Estate. In 1867,laying the foundation to the Sri Lanka's largest export industry for over centuries.Then he started a fully equiped factory opened with the development of the technology to operate all the processes in 1872 on the Loolecondera Estate. Twenty-Three pounds of Tea was produced by James Tailor in 1873 making the first international debut to London.


By visiting Loolecondera Estate people werre more interested in planting Tea and Tea planting grew rapidly because of that all over the hill country in 1880s. Coffee plantation had been converted to Tea Planting because Tea Planting become more luctrative alternative.In 1877 the SIROCCO tea dryer and in 1880 the tea rolling machines were developped with the development of the technology. Then the Central Tea Factory was built in 1884 on the FAIRYLAND ESTATE(Pedro) in Nuwaraeliya Sri Lnka.Ceylon Tea was sold at an astonishing price of LKR 36.15 per lb in 1891 at the London Tea auctons. At the age of 57 the Co-Founder of the Tea Planting Industry dies in 1892.The Colombo Tea Traders Association was founded in 1894 and it was followed by the formation of the Colombo Tea Broker's Association in 1896.



Co-Founder of the Tea Plantation


JAMES TAYLOR was the first Scottish Tea Planter who introduces Tea plantaion to Sri Lanka.In 1852 he fist stepped into Sri lanka and settled down in Loolecondera estate in Delthota.He worked with Scottish merchant Thomas Lipton to develop the Tea industry in Sri Lanka. More than half of his life time he lived in Sri Lanka making the Tea Industry more powerful than the other countries.In 1866 Taylor visited India to learn the basics of growing tea on plantations.After returning from India to Sri Lanka he started to plant Tea in Sri Lanka using the Loolecondera Estate in Kandy. In 1867 he began to plant Tea using 19 acres estate.Then he started a fully equiped tea factory in 1872 on the Loolecondera Estate.


In 1872 James Taylor started manufacturing packeted tea in Loolecondera Tea Estate.At this point he thought to write about his success saying "I HAVE A MACHINE OF MY OWN INVENTION BEING MADE IN KANDY FOR ROLLING TEA WHICH I THINK WILL BE SUCCESSFUL".In 1875 he has sent the first shipment of Ceylon Tea to the London Tea Auction.

Milestones in Tea Plantation



Sri Lanka has the most rich tapestry of climates which suits for Tea Plantation.Sri Lanka was the best cofee producer through world wide but today it has been blanketed with Ceylon Tea plantations.The most fragrant Tea growing regions are Dimbula,Nuwara Eliya,Ruhuna and Uva province in Sri Lanka. Heavy monsoon rains from May to September gives the all nutrients to the growth of the tea plant in Dimbula.In Dimbula the tea planters plant various types of Tea using this finest climate.Nuwara Eliya is also known ass the best tea growing district.The cooling climate gives the better products of tea.The harvest is done between February and March,July and August.
